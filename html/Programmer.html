In my senior year of high school, I partook in AP Computer Science, where I learned the basics of Java. I enjoyed the intellectual challenge the class provided but did not pursue the language further. In 2017, I participated in a Hackathon at Wayne State University. My partner and I went in with the intent to code an Alexa skill to help those with speech impairments practice speaking words aloud. The three-day event was full of late nights and hard work. Participating in the Hackathon gave me a sense of pride in my work and ignited a love for technology.

Recently I have taken it upon myself to dive deeper into the world of coding by practicing Python. I have enrolled myself in the Google IT Automation professional certification on Coursera and have already completed one of the six courses. I have also been practicing my Python skills on Codecademy. Both programs have improved my knowledge in the language, and I will continue with them. Although I am new to coding, I am eager to learn all I can to improve my skills.
